Assurance of salvation

A common argument that Christian missionaries and apologists throw at Islam is this idea that Islam teaches that salvation is based on works and that Muslims cannot be assured of their salvation in the afterlife. If the missionary argument is correct then we should find proof within the Quran and Sunnah that works are highlighted more than faith, and that our good deeds is what gets us into paradise. We should also see if Muslims will be punished in Hell the same way as disbelievers will be. However if one looks at the Quran and Sunnah objectively, they will see that Islam teaches the exact opposite of what the Christian apologists claim. Let’s begin shall we ———- Proof # 1 Lets see what the Quran says about the salvation of Muslims in the afterlife: 👇 Allah states in Quran 9:72: ”Allah has promised the believers, both men and women, Gardens under which rivers flow, to stay there forever, and splendid homes in the Gardens of Eternity, and—above all—the pleasure of Allah. That is ˹truly˺ the ultimate triumph.” (Quran 9:72) Allah also states in Quran 3:9: ”Our Lord! You will certainly gather all humanity for the ˹promised˺ Day—about which there is no doubt. Surely Allah does not break His promise.” (Quran 3:9) With these two verses in mind, we can do some simple hermeneutics and form this syllogism, Premise 1: Allah never breaks his promises (Quran 3:9) Premise 2: Allah promises Muslims heaven (Quran 9:72) Conclusion: Therefore Muslims are guaranteed heaven and thus have assurance on their salvation.
